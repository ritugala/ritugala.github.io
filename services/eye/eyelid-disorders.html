Eyelid Disorders

Your eyelids help protect your eyes. When you blink, your eyelids spread moisture over your eyes. Blinking also helps move dirt or other particles off the surface of the eye. You close your eyelids when you see something coming toward your eyes. This can help protect against injuries. Like most other parts of your body, your eyelids can get infected, inflamed, or even develop cancer. There are also specific eyelid problems, including Eyelids that turn in or out -Entropion and Ectropion (Surgery), Eyelids that droop-Ptosis(surgery), Abnormal blinking or twitching- Blepharospasm (botox), Eyelid laxity and drooping in old age ( Surgery-blepharoplasty), Eyelid swellings- chalazion, stye ,tumors ,warts (surgery/ removal), Blepharitis and meibomian gland dysfunction -conservative treatment.
